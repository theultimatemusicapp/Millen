Band Promotion Resources

Access comprehensive guides and templates to effectively promote your band and reach new audiences.

1. Optimise Your Spotify Profile

Professional Profile: Use high-quality images, a compelling bio, and links to your socials.

Artist Pick: Highlight your best or newest track.

Playlists Section: Create and feature your own playlists, mixing your songs with others in your genre to attract similar audiences.

Download Template

2. Leverage Spotify for Artists

Claim Your Profile: If you haven’t already, claim your Spotify for Artists profile to access analytics and playlist submission tools.

Submit to Spotify Editorial Playlists: Use Spotify for Artists to pitch unreleased songs for playlist consideration.

  • Focus on tracks that best represent your band.
  • Include details like the mood, genre, instruments, and influences when submitting.
Download Template

3. Reach Out to Playlist Curators

Find Independent Curators:

  • Use platforms like SubmitHub, PlaylistPush, or Soundplate.
  • Research playlist curators on Spotify and find their contact info (often available in playlist descriptions or through LinkedIn/social media).

Personalised Pitches:

  • Introduce your band, share a brief story about your song, and explain why it fits their playlist.
  • Keep it short and respectful.
Download Template

4. Promote Your Songs Organically

Social Media Presence: Use Instagram, TikTok, or Facebook to share clips of your music, behind-the-scenes content, or even snippets of your life balancing work and music.

Engage Your Fans:

  • Ask them to share your songs and add them to their playlists.
  • Create content encouraging fans to tag you in their stories while listening.

Collaborate with Local Artists: Feature or co-release tracks with other bands who have a following.

Download Template

5. Leverage Your Existing Network

Ask friends, family, and fans to add your music to their playlists, which helps with Spotify’s algorithm.

Tap into any local radio stations or music blogs to promote your music; they might know curators who can help.

Download Template

6. Use Paid Promotion Wisely

Social Media Ads: Target specific audiences, such as rock fans in your region or those who follow similar bands.

Playlist Placement Services: Be cautious and only use reputable services, avoiding anything that seems too good to be true (fake streams can harm your profile).

Download Template

7. Keep Releasing Music

Regular releases keep you on Spotify’s radar and improve your chances of being added to playlists.

Even if you’re busy, consider recording acoustic versions or releasing older live recordings.

Download Template

8. Focus on Your Story

Highlight your busy lives—work, family, music—as part of your unique narrative. People connect with authenticity.

Share short stories about your band’s journey in your song descriptions, social media posts, or even short videos.
